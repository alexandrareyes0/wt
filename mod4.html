Onshore vs. Offshore

This is the fourth module. This module explains the key difference between onshore and offshore wind. In this module you will learn the advantages and disadvantages of both sources of wind energy and form an opinion on which is more beneficial.

Main Focus

The main focus of this module is to understand what onshore and offshore wind are, respectively.

Goal

The goal of this section is to have an awareness of the benefits of offshore wind versus onshore depending on location and longevity.

Overview

The technology used in onshore and offshore wind turbines is mostly the same. Their differences include position, size, scale, and how the energy generated is transferred. Onshore and offshore wind farms have their own respective advantages and disadvantages, but their installation is also dependent on geographical location.

New Jersey has one onshore wind farm in operation. Opened in December 2005 and located in Atlantic City, the Jersey-Atlantic Wind Farm has a capacity of 7.5 MW.

Onshore Wind

Advantages and Disadvatanges of Onshore Wind

Advantages

Less Expensive

Infrastructure is less expensive than offshore wind due to size differences and less transportation/maintenance costs.

Quick Installation & Minimal Maintence

Can be installed within a few months and have low maintenance costs.

Environmental Impact

Onshore wind farms are typically set up near necessary manufacturing companies therefore eliminating carbon emissions created by transportation of wind turbine components.

Disadvatages

Inconsistent Wind

Wind speed and direction vary greatly on land due to blockages like buildings, mountains, etc. Due to this, onshore wind turbines cannot provide consistent power year round. They can only achieve about 2.5 MW compared to offshore’s average of 3.6 MW.

Intermittent Generation

Since onshore wind farms do not run year round, they require energy backups which may come from other renewable sources but also fossil fuel generators, which can have a negative impact on the environment.

Visual & Sound Impacts

Onshore wind farms are completely visible to the communities and businesses they are located near. Wind turbines also create a great deal of noise pollution which can be a hindrance to locals. In addition to this, onshore wind farms may pose a threat to birds.

Summary

Onshore wind farms are optimal for places without access to the coast, such as states located in the Midwest. The use of windmills onshore allows for the use of renewable energy despite not having access to the high speed winds offshore.

Onshore wind farms are less expensive due to smaller turbine size and minimal transportation for installation/maintenance, but produce less energy than offshore wind farms. Onshore wind farms are beneficial for inland locations with smaller populations, due to its visual/sound impacts.

Offshore Wind

Advantages and Disadvatanges of Offshore Wind

Advantages

Greater Energy Generation

Offshore wind speeds are greater than on land and don’t change direction as often. Due to this, fewer offshore wind turbines are needed to produce that same amount of energy as an onshore turbine.

Minimal Visual Impacts

Offshore wind farms are miles off of the coast meaning they are far from the local population, so the farms will have minimal to no noise pollution and are barely visible from shore. They will not interfere with the daily life of residents.

Optimal Location

The openness of the ocean allows for larger scale wind farms and in turn more clean energy production. The turbines can also have an increased size due to its location which allows for more wind energy to be captured.

Disadvantages

More Expensive

Offshore wind turbine components and installation are more expensive to their onshore counterparts due to the infancy of the industry in the US and lack of manufacturers. Installation of offshore wind turbines is more complex due to their location.

More Maintenance

Offshore wind farms require more maintenance than onshore wind due to sea waves and high wind speeds. They may also be more difficult to access to complete such repairs.

Visibility

Although OWF are located off the coast, they may still be visible depending on the depth they were installed in, the time of year, and weather conditions.

Summary

Due to the high concentration of people who live near the coast, within range to receive power from an offshore wind farm, approximately 80% of the American population, it will be extremely beneficial to these communities and to the nation as a whole. Renewable energy will cause a significant decrease in energy costs every year as more offshore wind farms are installed. Since wind speeds are high out to sea, the energy generated from these wind farms is larger than an onshore wind farm and in turn will be able to provide power to a large number of homes, businesses, etc..

In addition, offshore wind farms do not cause major disruptions to local populations due to their distance from shore, and ultimately will benefit these populations in the form of economic expansion.
