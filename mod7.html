What was the purpose of this course?

This course was designed to give anyone without previous knowledge of offshore wind all the components necessary to understand what it is, how it works, and why it is important.

Offshore wind is a growing industry that will become more prevelent within 10 years as more products arise and technology advances. Due to this, it is important to educate the people that this emerging industry will potentiall effect, which is 80% of the American population. Spreading knowledge of offshore wind will allow for a more seamless implmentation of this new clean energy source.

Module Summaries

Module 1: Introduction

Key Ideas:

1. Explains what offshore wind is
- A renewable source of energy that uses high speed winds from off the coast to create power from wind turbines
2. Explains the benefits offshore wind provides
- Helps the Environment
- Creates Jobs
- Will provide power to high percentage of Americans
-There is significant wind energy potential off the coast of the US

Module 2: How It Works

Key Ideas:

1. Explanation of how offshore wind collect and distribute power
- Turbines transform kinetic energy into electrical energy. The energy is sent to offshore substation then sent onshore.
2. Gives descriptions of the anatomy of an offshore wind turbine
- Large size
- Its parts: hub, blades, rotor, nacelle, tower, and foundation
3. Explains what offshore substations are
- Electricity generated from turbines is sent to substations through array cables where it is increased and stablized
- Power is sent through export cables onshore

Module 3: Current State

Key Ideas:

1. Wind History
- Wind has been used as a source of power as early as 5000 BC

Module 4: Onshore vs. Offshore

Key Ideas:

1. Onshore Wind
- Advantages: Less expensive, minimal maintence, quick installation, environmental impact
- Disadvatages: Inconsistent winds, intermittent generaiton, visual and sound impacts
2. Offshore Wind
- Advantages: Greater energy generation, minimal visual impacts, optimal location
- Disadvatages: More maintence, more expensive, visibility

Module 5: Types of Foudnations

Key Ideas:

1. Location
- The foundation type used in OWFs are dependent on its location
- Turbines required to be above crest levels of highest waves
2. Fixed Foundations
- foundations are secured into the seabed
- Gravity base, monopile, tripod, jacket
3. Floating Foundations
- foundations remain buoyant on water and kept in place using long cables attached to the seabed
- Tension leg, spar, semi submersible

Module 6: Life Cycle

Key Ideas:

1. Stages of an Offshore Wind Farm
- Preconstruction, installation, post construction, decommissioning
- On average, OWFs have a life span of about 25 years
2. Preconstruction
- A series of surveys are conducted on the proposed OWF location such as geophysical and geotechnical surveys
- This stage reveals any hazards or constraints that would prevent the project from moving forwards
3. Installation
- There are multiple stages within installation, such as: port, foundation installation, transition piece, turbine installation, substation installation, and cable laying operations
4. Post Construction
- Includes: commissioning and maintence operations
5. Decommissioning
- After about 25 years an OWF is considered for one of the following: Life extension, repowering, or a full decommission
